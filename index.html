About the platform

Artist-led filmmaking research, moving from site to site.

The Weight of Mountains is a platform and residency programme for artistic filmmaking. Every few years, an international cohort of filmmakers and film-based artists is selected through an open call to work on location with local communities, arts centres, and festivals.

Founded artistically by Melody Woodnutt, TWOM investigates how the human gaze, cognition, and body meet environment. Its films and conversations move through nature-culture symbiosis, psychogeography, landscape as character, and the truths and myths people impress upon the places they inhabit.

2025-2026 residency

From remote landscapes to an urban field of practice.

For its fourth residency, TWOM turns toward the urban environment of Ho Chi Minh City. The programme asks how filmmakers respond to a city where cultural sensitivity, contemporary film-based arts, and the nature-culture relationship demand a different agility than remote locations.

  • Three months accommodation, 2 December 2025 - 28 February 2026
  • A shared studio and hub workspace at A Farm International Art Residency
  • On-site discussions with local film and media curatorial voices
  • A symposium and local industry event hosted by TWOM and RMIT University Vietnam
  • End-of-residency screenings of film outcomes in Australia and Vietnam

Symposium programme

Screenings, discussion, and exchange across two days.

Day One 11 Dec

Panel discussion with The Weight of Mountains filmmakers

A 90-minute to 2-hour conversation moderated by TWOM Artistic Director Melody Woodnutt, introducing the long-form project and drawing out common research threads in the 2025-2026 cohort.

Screening at night

A selection of past and current TWOM films, with short filmmaker introductions where possible. Films noted in the deck include work by Terra Jean Long, Temujin Doran, Jean Jacques Martinod, Alisdair Bayne, Rachel Lin Weaver, and Melody Woodnutt.

Final films are subject to event permits and censorship clearance.

Day Two 12 Dec

Curatorial presentation, keynote, and facilitated discussion

A locally commissioned curator will connect TWOM's research with Ho Chi Minh City's filmmaking and media-art contexts, followed by a keynote from a prominent Vietnamese filmmaker.

Round table and networking

A facilitated two-hour exchange brings together graduating film students and visiting TWOM residents, with an early evening networking event for RMIT faculty, local filmmakers, and industry guests.

A feature screening of Terra Jean Long's Feet in Water, Head on Fire is proposed in partnership with Saigon Experimental Film Festival and RMIT.
